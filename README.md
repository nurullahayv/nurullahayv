## ◆ The short version

I work where **reinforcement learning, language, and the design of reasoning systems** meet — and I'm more interested in asking *which architectures could actually think more reliably* than in shipping another one. I start from a question, not a repository.

My training is electrical–electronics engineering — control systems, circuits, the discipline of making a dynamical system behave. But the questions kept drifting toward intelligence itself: how an agent learns *continually*, what makes it *want* to explore, and why a model reasons soundly one moment and hallucinates the next. So I followed them.

Most of what I build is scaffolding for an idea I'm chasing. The idea is the point.

---

## ◆ The questions I keep returning to

My doctoral direction sits on a single sentence — *next-generation AI architectures grounded in continual learning and intrinsic motivation, aimed at more reliable reasoning* — and resolves into three axes:

| Axis | The question underneath |
|------|------------------------|
| **Beyond the transformer** | Can nested / continual-learning paradigms (HOPE-style, *Nested Learning*) replace the static, frozen-weight model? |
| **Mechanistic interpretability** | What if we treated a model as a system to be *understood*, not merely trained? |
| **Reasoning &amp; hallucination robustness** | What makes a chain of inference hold — and what makes it quietly break? |

Underneath all three runs an older interest in **logic — classical and modal** — and in what Aristotelian structure might still teach machine reasoning. The philosophy is the inspiration; the engineering keeps it honest.

## ◆ Where the work lives right now

> **Reinforcement learning** is my sharpest instrument today.

PPO · TD3 · DDPG · curriculum learning · multi-agent RL — applied to control and guidance problems, and increasingly to the harder question of how an agent stays *curious* and keeps learning after deployment. I treat competitive ML as a route into academic work rather than an end in itself: each competition is a vehicle for a paper, a novel architecture, or a method worth defending.

## ◆ Selected research &amp; competition work

| Work | Idea in one line |
|------|------------------|
| **RAMSA** *(BirdCLEF+)* | Retrieval-Augmented Multi-Sample Aggregation for bioacoustics — borrowing MSA logic from structural biology and porting it to birdsong. |
| **Orbit Wars** | A full MARL / IPPO system — league-based self-play, deep ResNet + candidate-attention policy, reward shaping for adversarial orbital dynamics. |
| **KAN-PPO for BVR guidance** *(thesis)* | Kolmogorov–Arnold networks inside a PPO loop for beyond-visual-range guidance — bridging classical control with modern policy learning. |
| **DRfold2 extensions** *(Stanford RNA 3D Folding)* | Template-based, few-shot RNA structure prediction with cross-attention, FAISS retrieval, and MSA-style similarity search. |
| **PlantCLEF 2026** | Stable Diffusion + DINOv2 multi-scale test-time augmentation pipeline for fine-grained plant identification. |
| **Immune repertoire ML** *(AIRR-ML25)* | Multiple-instance learning fused with ESM-2 embeddings for repertoire classification. |
| **Brain aneurysm detection** *(RSNA 2025)* | Multi-stream CNN / Swin-Transformer inference for medical imaging. |
| **Diabetes modelling** *(Pima)* | A 13+ model comparison pipeline — classical to deep — with leakage fixes, RFE feature selection, and SHAP interpretation. |

*A through-line, in case it isn't obvious: structural biology, medicine, ecology, control — different domains, one method. Retrieve, aggregate, reason.*
